# Best invoicing software for freelancers

**Affiliate disclosure:** Some links in this guide are affiliate links. If you click and buy, we may earn a commission at no additional cost to you. This is a draft — review and edit before publishing.

Short overview:
Freelancers need invoicing that is quick to set up, handles taxes or VAT where relevant, and supports the payments their clients use. This draft covers Wave, FreshBooks, Zoho Invoice, Invoice Ninja, and PayPal Invoicing.

## Quick picks
- Wave — Best free option for solo freelancers on a budget
- FreshBooks — Best for time tracking + invoices
- Zoho Invoice — Best free tier with features
- Invoice Ninja — Best for self-hosters and flexible billing
- PayPal Invoicing — Best for PayPal-first clients

## Buying checklist for freelancers
- Free plan availability
- Payment gateways supported (PayPal, Stripe, local gateways)
- Recurring invoices & auto-reminders
- Time tracking and estimates (if needed)
- Ease of use for non-technical users
- Local currency & tax/VAT support

## Short tool summaries
### Wave
Pros:
- Free invoicing and accounting basics
- Easy setup
Cons:
- Limited advanced features for growing agencies
- Payments may not be available in all markets

### FreshBooks
Pros:
- Excellent UX and time tracking
- Good client communication features
Cons:
- Paid plans can be expensive for solo freelancers

### Zoho Invoice
Pros:
- Generous free tier
- Integrates with Zoho ecosystem
Cons:
- UI can feel cluttered for new users

### Invoice Ninja
Pros:
- Open-source option
- Highly configurable
Cons:
- Self-hosting requires technical setup

### PayPal Invoicing
Pros:
- Fast for PayPal users
Cons:
- PayPal fees; limited advanced invoice features

## Conclusion
Pick the tool that matches your payment methods and the features you use. Review the tool pages for direct links and up-to-date pricing.
